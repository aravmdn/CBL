# Project Overview — What CBL Is

_Snapshot date: 2026-05-26_

## The one-sentence version

A person stands in front of a camera; a Tibetan singing bowl is played; the bowl sound
and the person's heartbeat drive **living visuals** projected around them — colors,
ripples, and a glowing aura that react in real time.

## The story / concept

1. A person sits or stands in front of the installation.
2. Someone plays a **Tibetan singing bowl**. The deep, steady sound is calming.
3. As the person relaxes, their **heartbeat slows down**.
4. Sensors pick up two things: the **bowl's sound** (through a microphone) and the
   person's **heartbeat** (through a pulse sensor on an Arduino — hardware still
   arriving).
5. Software turns those signals into a live visual show around the person:
   - The **bowl sound** sets the **color and the rippling pattern** on screen.
   - The **heartbeat** sets the **pulse** — the visuals gently throb with each beat,
     and the color shifts depending on how fast the heart is beating (calm = violet,
     faster = orange).
   - The **camera** finds the person's body so all the effects wrap around them
     instead of floating randomly.

So the art reacts to a real, calming, physical experience. The calmer the person gets,
the more the visuals reflect it.

## Why a Tibetan singing bowl?

The bowl produces clear, sustained tones. Those tones can be matched to a colour scale
(the "chakra" colours — see the glossary). It also genuinely has a calming effect, which
is the whole point: the bowl changes the body, the body changes the visuals.

## A multi-disciplinary project (6 roles)

This is a TU/e CBL (Challenge-Based Learning) project for **Group 5**. The idea is that
**all six disciplines have a visible role** in the final piece:

- **Sound / physics** — the bowl's frequencies and how they map to colour and pattern.
- **Biology / biosignals** — the heartbeat and how a calming sound changes it.
- **Computer science** — capturing the signals and turning them into live visuals
  (this is Arav's part).
- **Visual art / design** — how the final image looks and feels.
- **(and the report / narrative side)** — explaining the meaning of the artwork.

## Key fact: the final presentation

The finished installation is presented on **19 June 2026**. It must run on a laptop
pointed at a person, and it must work **without internet** on the day.
